Virat Kohli, one of the leading cricketers globally, boasts an impressive array of records in One Day Internationals (ODIs). Renowned for his prolific batting, Kohli holds the distinction of being the fastest player to reach milestones such as 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, 11,000, and 12,000 runs in ODI cricket. His remarkable consistency is evident in his record-breaking feat of achieving the highest batting average in ODIs for a player with a minimum of 1,000 runs. Furthermore, Kohli has been instrumental as the captain of the Indian cricket team, guiding them through various ODI series. Notably, he holds the record for the fastest accumulation of 70 international centuries, encompassing both Tests and ODIs.

Virat Kohli, a standout cricketer in international Test matches,Kohli's Test records include being the fastest player to reach 8,000, 9,000, and 10,000 runs in Test cricket, underscoring his consistency and run-scoring ability. He has led the Indian Test team as captain, contributing significantly to their success in various series.

Virat Kohli, a cricketing maestro, has carved a remarkable legacy in the Indian Premier League (IPL), predominantly donning the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) jersey. Renowned for his prolific run-scoring abilities, Kohli holds a significant place in IPL history. In the 2016 season, he etched his name in the record books by amassing an unprecedented 973 runs, establishing a record for the most runs in a single IPL edition. Kohli consistently features among the tournament's leading run-scorers, showcasing his prowess as a top-order batsman.
